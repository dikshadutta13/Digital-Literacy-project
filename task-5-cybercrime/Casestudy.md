## Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud 

UPI payment fraud is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive banking details. A realistic example of this is when a college student receives a call from a fraudster pretending to be a customer care executive.

In a typical case, the scammer informs the victim about a cashback or refund and sends a payment request through a UPI app. The victim is then asked to enter their UPI PIN to “receive” the money. Step-by-step, the victim receives the request, trusts the caller, enters the PIN, and unknowingly approves a payment. As a result, money is deducted from their account instead of being received.

This type of fraud mainly targets students, online shoppers, and individuals who frequently use digital payment platforms, as they are more likely to trust such messages and act quickly.

The consequences include financial loss, mental stress, and possible misuse of personal information. In some cases, victims may also lose trust in digital payment systems.

This case highlights the importance of awareness. Users should remember that entering a UPI PIN is only required for sending money, not receiving it, and should always verify any suspicious requests.

## Fictional Scenario

A college student named Rahul received a phone call from someone claiming to be a customer care executive from a well-known payment app. The caller informed him that he had won a cashback reward and needed to follow a few steps to receive it. The scammer then sent a payment request through a UPI app and instructed Rahul to enter his UPI PIN to “claim” the reward.

Trusting the caller, Rahul followed the instructions and entered his PIN. Instead of receiving money, an amount of ₹5,000 was deducted from his bank account. When he tried to contact the number again, it was switched off. Later, he realized that he had been tricked into approving a payment request.

This type of fraud usually involves scammers pretending to be officials or service providers. They create urgency or offer rewards to manipulate victims into sharing sensitive information or approving transactions. Students and frequent digital payment users are often targeted due to their regular use of such apps.

The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in digital platforms. This scenario highlights the importance of awareness and understanding that UPI PIN should never be entered to receive money. Being cautious can help prevent such fraud.
