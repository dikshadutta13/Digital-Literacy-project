## Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud (Fictional Scenario)

UPI payment fraud is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive banking details. A realistic example of this is when a college student receives a call from a fraudster pretending to be a customer care executive.

In a typical case, the scammer informs the victim about a cashback or refund and sends a payment request through a UPI app. The victim is then asked to enter their UPI PIN to “receive” the money. Step-by-step, the victim receives the request, trusts the caller, enters the PIN, and unknowingly approves a payment. As a result, money is deducted from their account instead of being received.

This type of fraud mainly targets students, online shoppers, and individuals who frequently use digital payment platforms, as they are more likely to trust such messages and act quickly.

The consequences include financial loss, mental stress, and possible misuse of personal information. In some cases, victims may also lose trust in digital payment systems.

This case highlights the importance of awareness. Users should remember that entering a UPI PIN is only required for sending money, not receiving it, and should always verify any suspicious requests.
